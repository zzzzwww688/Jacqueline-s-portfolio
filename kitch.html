Ktisch MV

Inspiration

Listening to music and watching music videos is one of my hobbies. I really like the way kpop shoots mv. My dream is to be a singer, so I want to shoot my own mv. In the course of still & moving image, I cover this mv as my final project.

Creative Process

  • Storyboarding: Carefully crafted visuals to match the pacing and mood of the song.
  • Styling and Makeup: Designed outfits and makeup looks that complemented the MV's aesthetic.
  • Editing: Used advanced editing techniques to weave together a cohesive and compelling narrative.

Component

I worked with a fellow director at tisch. I was the scenarist director, an actor, video editor, stylists and makeup artistsin this video. We shot the video over three days in six locations in New York: Central Park, Times Square, dumbo, the Barbie pop-up, and more. As a director, I need to write the story, draw the storyboards and so on. As an actor, I need to learn this dance and interpret the emotions of the character.

takeaways

I am very happy and satisfied with this shooting. Even though the final film still has a lot of areas that can be improved, such as dance, editing techniques and so on. But this is my first attempt at directing and acting in a music video. It allowed me to see my potential and realize part of my dream. I hope I will have more opportunities to contact the entertainment industry and do more shooting in the future.

Challenges and Learnings

  • Challenges Faced: Managing multiple roles simultaneously, from directing to performing. Ensuring the team stayed on schedule across multiple shooting locations in New York City. Balancing technical elements like lighting, camera angles, and post-production editing.
  • Lessons Learned: Improved skills in visual storytelling through detailed storyboarding and creative direction. Developed a deeper understanding of character portrayal and emotional interpretation in front of the camera. Gained experience in project management and collaboration within a creative team.

Future Aspirations

  • Continue exploring music, film, and performance by creating more music videos.
  • Seek opportunities for professional collaborations and larger-scale productions.
  • Refine skills in choreography, editing, and direction for even more polished works.
